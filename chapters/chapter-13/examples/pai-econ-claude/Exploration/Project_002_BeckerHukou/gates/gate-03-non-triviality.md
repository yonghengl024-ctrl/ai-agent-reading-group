**Detailed findings:**

- **P_C2:** Standard economic intuition ("lower returns → lower investment") gives the WRONG directional answer for intermediate-ability rural children. The model machinery is essential: the result requires the multiplicative penalty (A7), the discrete-jump escape route (A9), and p₀ > 0 (A17) working together. Remove any one of these and the over-investment disappears. ORIGINAL relative to Arrow, Galor-Zeira, and the broader discrimination literature.

- **P_C3:** The direction of the gap in the under-investment regime (part i) is BORDERLINE — the direction is intuitive, though the exact formula requires the model. Parts (ii)–(iv) — the effect of p₀ on the over-investment range width, and the non-monotone aggregate gap — are NON-TRIVIAL. The composite proposition is acceptably non-trivial.

- **P_W1:** The positivity of welfare loss is intuitive (BORDERLINE), but the non-monotone θ-profile of ΔW — with a local maximum near the regime boundary θ̃ — is not guessable without the model. The result has genuine policy targeting content (intermediate-ability rural children bear the largest welfare burden).

- **P_W2:** Completely counter-intuitive. Any economist would predict that hukou reform improves welfare AND increases educational attainment. The model shows reform improves welfare AND reduces educational attainment for intermediate-ability rural students. This is a genuine non-trivial prediction suitable for empirical testing.
